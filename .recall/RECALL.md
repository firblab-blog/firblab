### What We Know For Certain

1. **Physical layer is fine.** NIC is 10G, link detected, no cable/hardware issue.
2. **Switch is fine.** Port 9 on switch-04 has correct Proxmox Trunk profile with native VLAN 10. Never lost its config.
3. **`vmbr1`/TrueNAS side works fine.** Not involved in the problem.
4. **VMs on lab-01 have working network.** Tagged VLAN traffic through vmbr0 works — confirmed by VM ARP traffic visible in tcpdump.
5. **ARP works at the NIC level.** tcpdump on `enp10s0f0np0` confirmed:
   - ARP requests leave the NIC ✓
   - ARP replies arrive back from 10.0.10.1 ✓
6. **The problem is entirely inside the bridge.** Frames arrive at `enp10s0f0np0` but are not delivered to the host management interface.

### Root Cause (Confirmed)
The switch sends ARP replies as **untagged** frames (native VLAN 10). When untagged frames arrive at `enp10s0f0np0`:
- The bridge applies the port's PVID to classify them
- `bridge-vids 2-4094` does **not** set a PVID, so default PVID=1 applies
- VLAN 1 is not in the 2-4094 allowed range → frames are **silently dropped**

### Unanswered Question
**Why doesn't `bridge vlan add dev enp10s0f0np0 vid 10 pvid` work?**  
Three possibilities:
1. `vmbr0.10` isn't properly receiving VLAN 10 frames from the bridge self port
2. Something in the live state (from Codex's `ip` commands) left `vmbr0.10` in a broken state even after `ifreload -a`
3. The UniFi switch with native VLAN 10 doesn't accept tagged VLAN 10 frames back from the host, so outbound ARP requests from `vmbr0.10` never reach the gateway
